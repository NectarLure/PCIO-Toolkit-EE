Evidence Boundary Statement

The real-organisation materials on this site are used only for T0 baseline diagnosis, formative field evaluation, and contextual fit checking. They do not include T1/T2 follow-up data, objective KPI tracking, control groups, or longitudinal operation records. They must not be interpreted as evidence of post-deployment improvement, causal effects, or realised ROI.

Not a causal performance study Not a commercial deployment platform ROI is scenario estimation only T0 baseline diagnosis

Research Purpose

PCIO Toolkit addresses smart-manufacturing diagnosis and decision support for resource-constrained organisations. It translates a staged capability logic into an executable, traceable, and retestable artifact. Expert review focuses on design requirements, functional structure, output logic, and evidence boundaries rather than on claims of organisational performance change.

DSR Positioning

This study is positioned as a Design Science Research artifact design and evaluation paper. The main contributions are the PCIO staged capability model, a diagnosis-to-PDCA tool architecture, and reusable design principles for SME-oriented smart-manufacturing diagnostic tools.

Expert Review Process

  1. Read the positioning and boundaries.Confirm that the study is a DSR artifact and formative evaluation study, not a causal empirical study.
  2. Review the artifact logic.Check whether the PCIO model, diagnostic loop, module structure, and design principles are consistent.
  3. Inspect the evidence package.Distinguish automated tests, synthetic demonstrations, and real T0 baseline cases.
  4. Review the two anonymised T0 cases.Assess whether baseline diagnosis, risk identification, KPI suggestions, and PDCA plans are useful and cautious.
  5. Complete the expert review form.Use the 1-5 Likert scale and open questions. This static site does not transmit or store form data.

Privacy Protection

The public site does not show original company names, employee information, real equipment identifiers, vehicle plates, customers, suppliers, precise addresses, or sensitive tank-area details. Cases are represented only as Case P and Case T, with anonymised summaries and generic risk/KPI statements.
